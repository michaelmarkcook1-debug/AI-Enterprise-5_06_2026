# Product Review Walkthrough

## Objective

Before adding more functionality, review the app as a product.

Walk the full experience:

```text
Query → Understand → Assess → Demonstrate → Monitor → Investor Tools
```

The goal is to test whether the platform feels like one coherent executive decision platform rather than a collection of useful but disconnected tools.

## Review Questions

### Overall Product

Ask:

1. Is the product purpose obvious within 10 seconds?
2. Does the navigation match the executive decision journey?
3. Does each tab answer one clear question?
4. Are the most valuable features visible early?
5. Is anything important buried too deep?
6. Are evidence, confidence and data freshness visible enough?
7. Does the product feel board-ready?
8. Does the product feel credible enough to cite?
9. Is the UI visually premium without becoming cluttered?
10. Does each page end with a clear next action?

## Page-by-Page Review

---

# Query Review

Query should answer:

> What is happening in the AI market?

Check:

- Does the page open with an executive market brief?
- Are market events grouped logically?
- Is the CIO implication visible?
- Are rankings/supporting charts lower down?
- Does the user understand what changed and why it matters?
- Are source/provenance labels clear?

Pass condition:

```text
A CIO can scan Query in 3 minutes and understand the market changes that require attention.
```

---

# Understand Review

Understand should answer:

> What is this vendor and where does it fit?

Check:

- Is the vendor universe easy to navigate?
- Is Atlas accessible but not overwhelming?
- Are vendor dependencies visible?
- Are capability scores backed by evidence?
- Are sustainability and encroachment risks visible?
- Are evidence grades explained?

Pass condition:

```text
A CIO or analyst can understand a vendor's role, capabilities, dependencies, risks and defensibility.
```

---

# Assess Review

Assess should answer:

> What should we do?

Check:

- Are the three assessment tiers obvious?
- Is Tier 1 quick?
- Is Tier 2 strategic?
- Is Tier 3 procurement-grade?
- Can outputs flow into Demonstrate?
- Does the assessment recommend a stack, not just a vendor?
- Are risk and sustainability included?

Pass condition:

```text
A user can move from organisation context to recommended AI stack and shortlist.
```

---

# Demonstrate Review

Demonstrate should answer:

> How do I defend this decision?

Check:

- Is Board Defence Score visible?
- Is CIO Confidence Score visible?
- Is the business case clear?
- Is cost of inaction clear?
- Are competitors/peers shown?
- Is the risk register credible?
- Are mitigations shown?
- Are assumptions explicit?
- Are reputation, pricing and uptake used as proof points?
- Can a board pack be generated?

Pass condition:

```text
A CIO can take the Demonstrate output into a board/investment committee meeting.
```

---

# Monitor Review

Monitor should answer:

> Is this still valid?

Check:

- Are active recommendations shown?
- Is recommendation drift visible?
- Are assumptions monitored?
- Are reassessment triggers clear?
- Are vendor/news/regulatory changes visible?
- Is there a clear action queue?

Pass condition:

```text
A CIO can see whether a previous AI decision still holds or requires reassessment.
```

---

# Investor Tools Review

Investor Tools should answer:

> Where should capital be allocated?

Check:

- Is it clearly separate from CIO procurement logic?
- Does it include category momentum?
- Does it include exposure risk?
- Does it include scenario simulation?
- Are financial estimates clearly labelled?
- Does it avoid presenting investment advice as certainty?

Pass condition:

```text
An investor can identify categories and vendors worth deeper diligence.
```
